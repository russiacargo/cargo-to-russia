Customs Clearance for Shipping from Dubai to Russia

A practical guide to clearing your cargo through Russian customs — required documents, import duties, prohibited items, and how to avoid common delays.

Customs clearance is the single biggest cause of shipping delays between Dubai and Russia. Understanding what Russian customs requires before your cargo ships can save you days of waiting and unexpected charges at the border.

This guide walks through the customs process step by step, whether you are sending commercial cargo, personal effects, or parcels from the UAE to Russia.

Required Documents for Russian Customs

Every shipment entering Russia must have the following documentation prepared accurately:

  • Commercial Invoice — must include item descriptions, quantities, unit prices, total value, and HS codes (Harmonized System codes)
  • Packing List — detailed breakdown of package contents, weights, and dimensions
  • Bill of Lading (sea) or Air Waybill (air) — issued by the carrier
  • Certificate of Origin — proves where goods were manufactured
  • Customs Declaration (GTD) — the formal Russian import declaration, usually filed by the customs broker
  • Import License — required for certain regulated product categories

Russian Import Duties and Taxes

Russia applies customs duties based on the HS code of the imported goods. The main charges include:

  • Import Duty — ranges from 0% to 20% depending on the product category
  • VAT (НДС) — standard rate of 20% applied on top of the customs value + duty
  • Excise Tax — applies to specific goods like alcohol, tobacco, and fuel

For personal parcels under a declared value of €200 and under 31 kg, no customs duty is charged. Above this threshold, a flat 15% duty applies.

Prohibited and Restricted Items

The following items cannot be shipped from Dubai to Russia or require special permits:

  • Weapons, ammunition, and military equipment
  • Narcotics and psychotropic substances
  • Counterfeit or pirated goods
  • Certain food products (meat, dairy without proper certification)
  • Plants and seeds (phytosanitary certificate required)
  • Medicines (limited to personal use with prescription)
  • Encryption devices and certain electronics (GOST certification may be required)

Common Customs Delays and How to Avoid Them

Most customs delays when shipping from Dubai to Russia are caused by:

  1. Incorrect HS codes — using wrong product classification codes triggers inspections. Always verify codes before shipping.
  2. Undervalued declarations — Russian customs flags shipments where declared values seem too low compared to market prices.
  3. Missing documents — incomplete packing lists or missing certificates of origin are the most common causes of holds.
  4. Restricted goods without permits — electronics and certain chemicals need GOST or EAC certification for Russian import.
