Shipping to Moscow from Dubai: Complete Guide

A complete guide to shipping cargo from Dubai to Moscow covering air freight, sea freight, courier options, customs clearance, transit times and costs.

Moscow is the primary destination for most cargo moving from Dubai to Russia. Whether you are sending commercial goods, personal effects, documents, or parcels, the route from Dubai to Moscow is the most established and frequently used shipping lane between the UAE and Russia.

This guide covers the main shipping methods, expected transit times, documentation requirements, and how to get the best rates for cargo from Dubai to Moscow.

Air Freight from Dubai to Moscow

Air cargo is the fastest option for shipping to Moscow from Dubai. Direct flights connect Dubai International Airport (DXB) and Al Maktoum International (DWC) to Moscow's Sheremetyevo (SVO) and Domodedovo (DME) airports daily.

Transit time: 2–3 business days for standard air freight, or same-day for express services. Air freight is ideal for urgent shipments, commercial samples, documents, electronics, and smaller cargo under 500 kg.

Typical cost: Starting from $12–15 per kg depending on cargo weight, dimensions, and urgency. Rates are generally lower for shipments above 100 kg.

Sea Freight from Dubai to Moscow

Sea freight from Dubai to Moscow typically routes through the port of St. Petersburg or Novorossiysk. From Jebel Ali Port in Dubai, container ships sail to these Russian ports, and the cargo is then transported overland by rail or truck to Moscow.

Transit time: 25–35 days total (including inland transit to Moscow). Sea freight is best for heavy cargo, bulk shipments, vehicles, machinery, and large commercial orders where cost matters more than speed.

Typical cost: Starting from $1,800 for a 20ft container. LCL (Less than Container Load) rates start from approximately $80–120 per cubic metre.

Courier and Parcel Service to Moscow

For smaller shipments, parcels, and documents, a courier service from Dubai to Moscow provides door-to-door delivery with tracking. This is a popular option for personal items, gifts, e-commerce orders, and business documents.

Transit time: 5–10 business days. Express courier options can deliver in 3–5 days.

Typical cost: Starting from $25 flat fee plus $8–12 per kg. Parcels under 5 kg typically cost $45–75.

Customs Clearance for Moscow Shipments

All cargo entering Russia through Moscow requires customs clearance. The key documents you need include:

  • Commercial Invoice — with accurate value and item descriptions
  • Packing List — detailing contents, weight, and dimensions
  • Bill of Lading or Airway Bill — issued by the carrier
  • Certificate of Origin — if required for specific goods
  • Import permits — for restricted categories (food, chemicals, electronics)
