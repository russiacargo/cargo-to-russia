Shipping Time from Dubai to Russia: How Long Does It Take?

A breakdown of transit times from Dubai to Russia by air freight, sea freight, and courier — including times to Moscow, St. Petersburg, Vladivostok, and other cities.

One of the most common questions we receive is: "How long does shipping from Dubai to Russia take?" The answer depends on three factors — the shipping method, the destination city in Russia, and whether customs documents are prepared correctly.

These are estimated transit times under normal conditions. Customs clearance typically adds 1–3 days depending on documentation accuracy.

Air Freight Transit Times

Air freight from Dubai to Russia is the fastest shipping method. Direct flights from Dubai (DXB/DWC) to Moscow (SVO/DME) operate daily, with connecting flights to other Russian cities.

Standard air freight takes 2–3 business days to Moscow. Add 1–2 days for cities further from Moscow like Novosibirsk, Yekaterinburg, or Vladivostok.

Express air cargo can deliver to Moscow in as fast as 24–48 hours, ideal for urgent documents, medical supplies, or time-critical business shipments.

Sea Freight Transit Times

Sea freight from Jebel Ali Port (Dubai) to Russian ports takes considerably longer but is much more cost-effective for heavy or bulk cargo:

  • To Novorossiysk (Black Sea): 18–22 days sailing + 3–5 days inland to Moscow
  • To St. Petersburg (Baltic Sea): 25–30 days via Suez Canal
  • To Vladivostok (Pacific): 35–40 days via Indian Ocean
  • To Kaliningrad (Baltic): 28–32 days

Add 3–7 days for customs clearance and inland transportation to the final delivery address. Container availability and port congestion can also affect timelines.

What Affects Shipping Time?

Several factors can speed up or delay your Dubai-to-Russia shipment:

  1. Document accuracy — incorrect or missing paperwork is the #1 cause of delays
  2. Customs inspection — random inspections can add 2–5 days
  3. Destination city — Moscow and St. Petersburg are fastest; remote Siberian cities take longer
  4. Cargo type — restricted items need additional permits and clearance time
  5. Season — year-end holidays and Russian New Year (Dec-Jan) slow down all services
